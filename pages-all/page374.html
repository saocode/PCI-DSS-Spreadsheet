5. Address the additional risk imposed by not adhering to the PCI DSS requirement.
6. Address the requirement currently and in the future. A compensating control cannot address a requirement that was missed in the past
(for example, where performance of a task was required two quarters ago, but that task was not performed).
The assessor is required to thoroughly evaluate compensating controls during each annual PCI DSS assessment to confirm that each
compensating control adequately addresses the risk that the original PCI DSS requirement was designed to address, per items 1-6 above.
To maintain compliance, processes and controls must be in place to ensure compensating controls remain effective after the assessment is
complete. Additionally, compensating control results must be documented in the applicable report for the assessment (for example, a Report
on Compliance or a Self-Assessment Questionnaire) in the corresponding PCI DSS requirement section, and included when the applicable
report is submitted to the requesting organization.
